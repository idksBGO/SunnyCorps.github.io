Computer Science As Discipline

Brief History

Computer science is a recent discipline that talks about algorithmics, mathematics, science and many more. There is no birthday to when Computer science became a discipline as a lot of people believe in various dates. One could say that it was born in the 1600s or the 1800s, however a lot would agree that it started in the 1960s. It was spearheaded by both Alan Turing and Kurtz Godel with their mathematical and algorithmic works, which was the same as Ada Lovelace’s algorithms 60 years earlier. Soon later other mathematicians such as John von Neumann would help in the evolution of Computer science into the present one we have.


What is Computer Science?

Computer science encompasses much more than just programming software and applications. It is a broad field that includes areas such as Hardware, Algorithms, Data analysis, Problem-solving, Theoretical Concepts, Security, Software Testing, and many more.


Primary Focus

In the field of Computer Science it primarily focuses on developing and programming software and applications either on a practical level or in a theoretical level. Those who chose to program at a theoretical level hope that it ends as a practical.


A Science for the Artificial

Computer science is a science of the artificial, which means it's mostly involved with human made objects/processes such as computers, machinery, software, applications and other man-made structures that involves technology.



Five Computing Disciplines & Majors

Computer Science

A study of computers that includes their evolution, applications, operations, and architecture. It uses both practical and theoretical aspects of engineering, programming, electronics, and Information Technology. It is subject to a wide range of overlapping disciplines and systematic studies of computing processes.


Computer Engineering

A study of computers that includes their evolution, applications, operations, and architecture. It uses both practical and theoretical aspects of engineering, programming, electronics, and Information Technology. It is subject to a wide range of overlapping disciplines and systematic studies of computing processes.


Software Engineering

A recent creation proposed in 1968 and has become a fully fledged discipline in the field of engineering. It provides various methods in handling complexities in a software system that enables the development of reliable software that can be used in civilian, machinery, businesses, and the military.


Information Systems

It deals with the storage, collection, processes, analysis, and dissemination of information for a specific task and purpose. Most of the applications of Information Systems are used in businesses as confidential information will be stored and protected like user personal data and the staff’s personal own data. Data can now easily be accessed and managed in one centralized area instead of multiple amounts of data storages located in different areas of the world.


Information Technology

It is a technology of storing, managing, structuring network infrastructure, and acquiring information from different areas of the planet. This revolution gave qualitative changes compared to the traditional way of acquiring and storing information as well as in production processes and business activities. It has a profound impact in the fields of medicine, education, governance, and others.

    Number Systems

    What is a Number System?

    Dating back approximately 5,000 years in ancient Babylonia, number systems were in use, though not in the same manner as today. Instead, they employed a weighted positional system.


    Mathematics defines it as a method for representing, expressing, and writing numerical values based on their measurements. This system employs symbols to depict different types of numbers within a specific set and has been utilized for thousands of years to aid in various mathematical calculations.



    Types of Number Systems

    What are the Types of Number Systems?

    Here is the list of the type of Number Systems:

  • Binary Number System
  • Octal Number System
  • Decimal Number System
  • Hexadecimal Number System

    • Binary Number System

    It resembles the Decimal system, but it utilizes only two numeral symbols, which is why it’s called “Binary.” These two numbers are 0 and 1.


    Binary code is extensively utilized in electronic computers because all data inputted is automatically transformed into binary format. Computers rely on binary calculations, which is why they convert all data into this format for efficient storage, management, and processing.


    Octal Number System

    The octal number system operates on a base-8 framework, utilizing the digits 0 through 7. This system represents a unique numerical set. When decomposing octal numbers, they can be converted into binary form by grouping three binary digits (0s and 1s) together to create a single octal unit.


    Decimal Number System

    A number system that employs digits from 0 to 9 operates on a base-10 framework. In this system, each digit's value is determined by its position, with each digit representing ten times the value of the digit to its immediate right. For instance, the number "5634" can be expressed mathematically as follows: (5 x 1000) + (6 x 100) + (3 x 10) + (4 x 1).


    Hexadecimal Number System

    The hexadecimal system utilizes 16 digits to represent base 16 numbers, encompassing digits from 0 to 15. Values exceeding 9 are denoted by letters rather than numeric symbols; for instance, 10 is represented as A, 11 as B, and this pattern continues up to 15. Furthermore, in contrast to the octal system, hexadecimal numbers are grouped in binary form using four digits instead of three.



    Significant Uses

    Binary Number System

    A system that we utilize in our daily lives to perform various tasks, including accounting, measurement, purchasing, weighing, conversions, and more.


    Octal Number System

    This system is integral to all the electrical devices we utilize daily. It enables computers to efficiently process and store the data that is inputted into them.


    Decimal Number System

    This method is utilized for convenience when handling large volumes of binary numbers, facilitating easier reading and management of extensive data sets. Various programming languages also use this system.


    Hexadecimal Number System

    It is employed in Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) arithmetic to enable programmers to achieve values that exceed those represented in Binary-Coded Decimal (BCD). Additionally, it serves to simplify binary numbers, enhancing their readability for human comprehension.

    Computer Hardware

    What is a Computer?

  • An electronic device that operates under the control of various programs. These programs, often referred to as software, enable a computer to perform a wide range of tasks. This is why it is used for a variety of purposes and tasks that involve difficulty to the human mind or work.
  • The computer now in the modern age is still the same computer that was used before in military operations before it became public. It still retains its basic fundamentals in the modern age but rather a more compact, powerful, and faster version of the 20th century.
  • However the computer is not only a singular component but rather a multitude of components that turns it into the computer we always use and know about. These components are known as External Components and Internal Components.

    • External Components

    These are outside computer hardware components that are required for the operation of the computer and enhances the experience in using a computer. These devices are also called peripheral devices. Examples of these devices are:


    Monitor

    A device that displays the information from the Computer.

    Printers

    A device that prints data from the computer based on the user’s command.

    Keyboard

    A device used for inputting text, characters, numbers, etc. into the computer.

    Mouse

    A device that lets you control, point, direct, click, and many more that lets you do a lot of things inside a computer.

    Cameras

    A device that is use to project the image of the user into the mainstream media and computer.

    Scanners

    A device used to scan items in the physical world and turn it into a form of data in the digital world.



    Internal Components

    These are internal computer hardware components that are a requirement for the functioning of the computer. These inner components are located inside the PC box and harbors the necessary equipment, functions, and basic needs of the computer. Examples of these internal components are:


    Central Processing Unit (CPU)

  • Acts as the brain of the computer.
  • Performs calculations and executes instructions.
  • Consists of the Control Unit (CU), Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU), and Registers.

    • Motherboard

  • A large circuit board connecting all hardware components.
  • Houses the CPU, RAM, and other critical components.

    • Random Access Memory (RAM)

  • Temporary, volatile memory used for data that is actively being used by the CPU.

    • Storage Devices

  • Hard Disk Drive (HDD): Magnetic storage for large volumes of data.
  • Solid State Drive (SSD): Faster, more durable, and efficient storage.

    • Power Supply Unit (PSU)

  • Converts electrical power from a wall outlet into usable power for the computer.

    • Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

  • Handles rendering of graphics, important for gaming and visual tasks.


    • Software
  • Software is the collection of digital programs and operating systems that enable the computer hardware to perform specific tasks. It is the opposite of hardware that it lacks the physical aspects that a hardware component has. Rather it is in the form of an application, scripts, and programs that executes and runs on a device.
  • A software can be divided into two main types: System Software and Application Software.

    • System Software

  • It consists of a variety of programs that support the operation of a computer; one characteristic in which most system software differs from application software is machine dependency.
  • It is widely associated with configuration of files and documentation.
  • An example of a System Software would be a SCADA system that has been in regular use. It is equipment that can outlast the creator of the program itself and has been used in different industrial processes and data gathering.
  • Other examples of System Software are:

    • Operating Systems

    A software that manages the PC as it provides the basic functions of the computer. It also has the ability to write and read data from the user's storage discs as well as display your current peripheral device setup.

    Device Drives

    This software supports the operating system in recognizing different kinds of hardware systems. It also helps the operating system in integrating the support of these hardware devices to ensure that the operating system uses the full capability of the hardware component.

    Firmware

    A special kind of software because it is primarily stored in chipsets that are non-volatile. It provides the computer with basic instructions for how the hardware of the computer may be operated. It is also used in different hardwares, examples are keyboards, video cards, sound cards, etc.


    Application Software

    A type of software that is written to perform a particular function of general utility. All of the time user's will come across these types of softwares as these are programs the people use everyday. Examples of these programs are:


    Productivity Software

    Examples: Word processors (Microsoft Word), spreadsheet programs (Excel), and presentation software (PowerPoint).

    Media Software

    Includes photo and video editing tools, such as Adobe Photoshop or VLC media player.

    Programs like Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, etc., for web browsing.

    Different Types of Motherboard & Their Specifications

    The Motherboard is the main system component that helps in powering your whole computer system. It is one of the most important parts of the PC that helps it in communicating between internal parts of the computer and it also serves as a plugger for peripheral devices. Basically it's the backbone of the computer that connects all internal parts of the computer.


    The motherboard is available in various types known as "form factors." These specifications are essential for determining which motherboard is compatible with your computer system. Typically, more compact PC cases accommodate smaller motherboards, while larger cases usually take bigger motherboards. The difference between these sizes is the number of plug-ins and expansion slots they offer.



    Different Types of Motherboards

    AT Motherboard

    AT (Advance Technology) Motherboard is an old motherboard type that was popular back in the 1980s all the way to the early 1990s. It has become insignificant ever since in 1997 as computers have replaced their AT Motherboards with ATX Motherboards. What made the AT Motherboard popular back then was its standardization, expansion, and overall flexibility. Majority of the time used in small wide servers and fit inside an AT PC casing.


    ATX Motherboard

    The most common or the standard motherboard in today’s age. It was created by Intel in order to have more flexibility and support for more processors. It replaced the AT Motherboard as the standard motherboard for PC usage back in 1997. It's pretty much an upgraded and better version of the old AT motherboard.


    BTX Motherboard

    A motherboard that was created by Intel and was supposedly the replacement of the ATX motherboard. It addresses the issues that the ATX motherboard has like heat management and power consumption. However its widespread usage was unsuccessful as people favored the ATX motherboard.


    Extended-ATX Motherboard

    A variant type of the ATX motherboard and is the biggest among the variants. This motherboard is specifically designed to build an extremely powerful computer. These kinds are used in high-end gaming as well as in other works such as graphic designers and video editors.


    LPX Motherboard

    Introduced by a company known as Western Digital, it is a low-profile motherboard for slimline PC cases. It was widely used back in 1987 with its introduction up until the late 1990s. This is when the release of the Pentium II drastically reduces in usages because of the requirements that the Pentium II needs that the LPX motherboard cannot meet. It was later discontinued in 1998 and NLX was supposed to be its replacement.


    Micro ATX Motherboard

    As the name suggests, it is a smaller and more compact version of the standard ATX motherboard. Introduced back in 1997, and was used as a budget computer for the people who cannot afford the regular computer with the standard ATX motherboard. However this motherboard doesn’t have the same flexibility as the standard and has no room left for expansion and customization.


    Mini-ITX Motherboard

    A variation of the ITX motherboard, It uses very little power and fans are not necessary to keep it cool. It only has one PCI slot for expansion cards and can be used anywhere which makes it a convenient choice for a PC.


    Mini-ATX Motherboard

    A variety of the ATX Motherboard and its a much smaller Micro-ATX motherboard. It has the same uses as the Micro-ATX, used for compact computers in small spaces such as automobiles, network devices, etc.


    Pico BTX Motherboard

    A smaller variant of the BTX motherboard. This motherboard is smaller compared to other micro-sized motherboards. Its use is for small and compact computers as well as for space-saving situations.


    Flex-ATX Motherboard

    It was released back in 1999 and was created by Intel as an additional specification for the Micro-ATX motherboard and is the smallest variation of the ATX family. Its intended design was to create more variations of new PC designs, especially for budget PCs.


    NLX Motherboard

    A low-profile motherboard created by Intel and other collaborators back in 1987. It was designed as a replacement for the nonstandard LPX motherboard. It contains new features and new supports for the latest of technologies. However the NLX motherboard went into the same route as the BLX motherboard, it was never widely accepted by the people.

    Comparative Study on Different Types of Computers

    A computer is an electronic device designed to store and process data. It can program and manipulate symbols according to the applications or tasks assigned by the user. It has the capability of solving complex equations in just seconds or minutes, and it efficiently performs most user tasks quickly and reliably.


    With the features and capabilities of the computer, it is now utilized globally because of its versatility in various sectors, both public and personal. It has significantly influenced daily human activities and brought about changes in numerous fields, including government, education, medicine, research, and more.



    History of Computers


    First Generation of Computers (1945-1955)

    Computers were not mainstream and were restricted to only military usages. In this generation computers were different to its modern counterparts, it was characterized by its use of vacuum tubes as its main component making it relatively huge. At this time, computers were unreliable, had a high rate of failure, were expensive, and had a ton of cooling problems.


    First Generation of Computers (1955-1965)

    This decade invented one of the main components that would define the computer, the transistor. The transistor is the fundamental building block when it comes to computer circuitry, it allows the computer to operate in a binary system as the transistor acts as the switches representing both 1 and 0. Furthermore the transistor was a major replacement for the vacuum tubes as it was seen as smaller, faster, and more reliable. Another thing that was invented was programming languages, specifically COBOL (Common Business Oriented Language) and FORTRAN (Formula Translator). Magnetic tapes were also created as a storage device.


    Third Generation of Computers (1965-1975)

    Transistors were upgraded and integrated into the IC commonly known as Integrated circuits. It is a small silicon chip whose power requirement is extremely small resulting in computers being faster, smaller, and reliable than before. Additionally Magnetic tapes were replaced with the Magnetic discs.


    Fourth Generation of Computers (1976-Present)

    VLSI technology was developed and has become a crucial element in the creation of the modern devices we rely on today. This innovation enabled the production of smaller, cost-effective, yet powerful computers, leading to the development of microprocessors. Also referred to as CPUs, microprocessors are a vital component of computers, as it incorporates key components such as the Arithmetic and Logic Unit, registers, and the control unit.


    Additionally, during this period, significant inventions like the command-driven interface, used for computer interaction, and the Graphical User Interface (GUI), which made the computer more user-friendly.


    Fifth Generation of Computers (1990-Present)

    Computers in this generation are to be one of the most fastests and more resembles our current 21st century computers. It has the ability to perform parallel processing and the concept of A.I. (Artificial Intelligence) was introduced to allow devices to operate on themselves.
